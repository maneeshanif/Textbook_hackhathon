# 3.5 Chapter Summary

## Key Concepts

### Vision Systems
- **RGB Cameras**: High-resolution images for object recognition
- **Depth Sensors**: Stereo cameras (triangulation) and ToF (time-of-flight)
- **Lidar**: Long-range, accurate 3D mapping with laser scanning

### Force and Inertial Sensors
- **Force/Torque Sensors**: Measure contact forces for ZMP calculation and compliant control
- **IMU**: Accelerometer + Gyroscope â†’ Orientation estimation with sensor fusion
- **Complementary Filter**: Fuses noisy accelerometer with drifting gyroscope

### Actuators
- **BLDC Motors**: Efficient (85-95%), precise, used in most modern humanoids
- **Hydraulic**: High power-to-weight but complex maintenance
- **Series Elastic Actuators**: Compliant, good for safe interaction

## Technical Formulas

### Stereo Depth

$$
Z = \frac{f \cdot B}{d}
$$

### Motor Torque

$$
\tau_{\text{output}} = G \cdot K_t \cdot I
$$

### SEA Torque Estimation

$$
\tau = K_s (\theta_{\text{motor}} - \theta_{\text{output}})
$$

### Complementary Filter

$$
\theta_{\text{fused}} = \alpha \cdot \theta_{\text{gyro}} + (1 - \alpha) \cdot \theta_{\text{accel}}
$$

## Design Trade-offs

| Component | High Performance | Low Cost / Simple |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Vision** | Lidar + RGB + Depth | Single RGB camera |
| **IMU** | 9-DOF with Kalman filter | 6-DOF with complementary filter |
| **Actuator** | Hydraulic (high power) | BLDC (efficient, reliable) |
| **Force Sensor** | 6-axis F/T sensor | Single-axis load cell |

## Real-World Systems

### Boston Dynamics Atlas
- **Vision**: Stereo cameras + lidar
- **IMU**: High-rate (1 kHz) for balance
- **Actuators**: Hydraulic (for dynamic jumps)
- **Force Sensors**: 6-axis in feet

### Tesla Optimus Gen 2
- **Vision**: 8 cameras (no lidar)
- **IMU**: 6-DOF in torso
- **Actuators**: BLDC motors with harmonic drives
- **Force Sensors**: Torque sensing in hands (11 DOF)
